Frequently Asked Questions

To begin the process for academic accommodations (such as testing, note-taking, and assistive technology accommodations), complete the Accessibility Services & Accommodations Intake Registration Form.

If you need accommodations for a short-term condition (such as an injury, concussion, or surgery), please complete the Temporary Disability Registration Form instead.

If you are looking for housing accommodations, please complete the Housing Accommodation Registration Form.

If you would like more information about housing accommodations, you can review the Housing Accommodations Policy.

The General Document Guidelines document lists the documentation necessary for the ASA staff to make accommodation decisions.

Types of acceptable third-party documentation may include, but are not limited to: psychological evaluations, psychoeducational assessments, medical evaluations, diagnosis letters, and neuropsychological evaluations. In addition, a record of past accommodations may be acceptable, such as a 504 Plan or an Individualized Education Plan from secondary school. For graduate students, a formal letter from a disability professional indicating accommodations used in undergraduate may be accepted as well.

Students can also have their provider complete the Disability Provider Form.

If you would like more information about housing accommodations, you can review the Housing Accommodations Policy.

One difference as you transition to college is the role your parents play in requesting and evaluating accommodations. At St. Bonaventure, YOU are in control of your academic requests, and you are the best source of information. Your parents are a great support, but you are responsible for requesting accommodations and self-advocacy.

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) requires that students give their express consent for the university to share any personal or academic information with parents or guardians. We can’t talk with your parents without your written consent.

Signing a FERPA waiver does not allow parents to arrange accommodations on their child's behalf, nor does it mean we will reach out to a student's parents. We will always contact you about your progress and accommodations.

If you would like us to have permission to share information with a parent, guardian, or third party, complete the Consent for Release of Confidential Information Form.

If you would like to appeal testing or academic accommodation decisions, please contact: asa@sbu.edu.

Use the Exam Scheduling Form to schedule your exam in the testing center.

Please complete your forms 3–5 business days before the exam to give the ASA office time to prepare for you.

You should schedule your exam to be at the same time as your class. If the class testing time conflicts with another of your classes or with the ASA office hours (exam start times are between 8am–2pm), please contact our office and your professor to find an appropriate alternate start time.
